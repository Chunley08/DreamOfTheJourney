The File

How He Got Here
⚠ Classified ⚠ Character Backstory — Spoilers — Click to Open

Shane grew up in a house that always felt smaller than it was. His mom — Nina Halbrook — was every good thing in the world stitched into one woman. Patient. Warm. The kind of person who'd give you the last piece of bread on the table and pretend she wasn't hungry. She loved her husband. That was her sin.

Walter Halbrook wasn't a husband so much as a parasite in a man's clothes. Cheated like he was being paid for it. Gambled the rent. Gambled the groceries. Gambled the medicine money. Came home reeking of someone else's perfume and expected dinner waiting. Nina worked three jobs to plug the holes he kept punching in their life. Shane watched. Shane remembered.

By the time Shane was fourteen, he'd already promised himself he'd never end up like his old man. By fifteen, he had a bigger problem — Nina got sick. Cancer. The kind that doesn't bargain. He spent the next year watching her shrink in a hospital bed while Walter couldn't make it today, son, and Shane held her hand for the both of them. She died when he was sixteen, alone.

Shane left that night. Didn't pack. Didn't say goodbye. A week later he was on the back of a stranger's bike, and that's how the next chapter started — patches, fists, a road name he won't tell you, and a list of things he doesn't bring up at parties. Murder. Drugs. Worse. You don't ride with that crowd without earning your way in.

Then he heard music that did something to his chest. He picked up a bass. Met the Snake Skins. Set the bike down — figuratively. He never sold the bike.

Does it bother him? Any of it? Not really. It happened. He was there. That was him. You can spend the rest of your life apologizing for being a man who survived, or you can play your fucking bassline. Shane chose the bassline.
