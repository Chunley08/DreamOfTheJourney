The Long an' Short of It

Who's Askin'?

Name's Rory. O'Malley, aye — Tommy's youngest, before ye ask, an' everyone always asks. Six foot four, red hair I keep longer than me Da'd like, an' a face that's gotten me into more beds an' more brawls than I could count if ye gave me a week an' a clear head, which ye won't, 'cause I've neither.

I can't sit still. Never could. Me mind goes ten ways at once an' me body goes chasin' all of 'em — I'll lose the thread o' what I'm sayin' halfway t'rough a sentence an' not even mind. But. Sit me down at a card table or square me up in a fight, an' somethin' in me head goes quiet. Locks in. That's the only time it's ever still, an' I'll let ye guess what that says about me.

I'll not lie to ye about lyin' — I do it easy as breathin', an' I'm terrible good at it. Fast mouth, faster hands, an' a smile that's talked me out o' more trouble than me fists ever got me into. I run the bootleg for the family 'cause it's easy money an' most o' the work is just makin' sure the right fellas look the other way. Lazy by nature, me. Do as little as I can get away wit' an' not a t'ing more.

That's the charmer. Buy us a drink an' that's the only Rory ye'll ever meet. Most fellas are lucky enough to stop there.

The File

Birmingham Constabulary — O'Malley, R. ⚠ Spoilers — the thing I don't talk about

Ye want the real story? Fine. But ye don't get to look at me different after, understand?

I was always in Declan's shadow, except I was never quiet enough to stay in it. Always the one who started the trouble, never the one who finished it. He took the beatings. He took the blame. An' I let him, every single time.

Then I killed a man. Seventeen, I was. Messin' about wit' some fella's wife. The husband found out, came at me in a pub, an' the fight went bad fast. I hit him an' I didn't stop. Turned out he was a judge's son. That changed everythin'.

Tommy sat me down. Said Declan would take the fall. Declan would go inside. Me Da knew I wouldn't survive prison — too reckless, too mouthy, too young. Declan could take it. So Declan did. Wit'out a word of complaint. Did the time for a thing I did, an' he's never once thrown it in me face.

Tommy's never spoken of it since. That silence says everythin' there is to say. An' me? I went right on bein' exactly what I was. That's the part I can't outrun. Not the body. The brother.
